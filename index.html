What Are Peptides?

Peptides are short chains of amino acids — the building blocks of proteins — that play critical roles in virtually every biological process. Unlike large proteins, their compact structure allows them to interact with specific receptors with high precision.

In research settings, synthetic peptides are used to study signaling pathways, tissue repair mechanisms, metabolic regulation, and cellular aging. Each compound in our catalog has been selected for its distinct research profile and compound stability.

Research Guide

Reconstitution Guide

Step-by-step instructions for preparing your lyophilized peptides

1

Gather Supplies

You will need: the lyophilized peptide vial, BAC Water (bacteriostatic water), sterile syringe, and alcohol swabs. All equipment should be sterile.

2

Prepare the Vial

Wipe the rubber stopper of both the peptide vial and the BAC Water vial with an alcohol swab. Allow to dry for 30–60 seconds before proceeding.

3

Add BAC Water Slowly

Insert the syringe into the BAC Water vial and draw the required amount. Inject slowly into the peptide vial along the inside wall of the glass — never directly onto the powder.

4

Dissolve Gently

Gently swirl (do not shake or vortex) the vial until the powder is fully dissolved. The solution should be clear. If it appears cloudy, discard it.

5

Store Properly

Once reconstituted, store the vial at 2–8°C (refrigerator). Use within 30 days. Lyophilized (unreconstituted) peptides store at –20°C for long-term stability.
